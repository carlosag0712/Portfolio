About...

With proven abilities in large program management, I have prudently led projects of high complexity and risk, recruited and mentored teams for their enthusiasm and productivity, and monitored contractual obligations for compliance.

Throughout the years, I've come to realize that the more technical an agile delivery leader is, the better he or she will server their teams. There is a case to be made about non-technical agile leader likelyhood of being successful. I've been a non-technical delivery leader for the last decade. However, as complexity increases so will the moving parts. This creates an urgent need for the team to interact with leaders instead of managers.

While I do agree that being too technical could make an agile leader take their eyes off of the "big picture" not being technical hinders their ability to serve the team and the business as a conduit of requirements and expectations. This has turned me into a strong advocate that agile leaders should, at least, strive to understand the fundamentals of computer science problem-solving, DevOps and actively participate in architectural discussions.
